Commercial & TI

Commercial Construction & Tenant Improvements in Santa Cruz

Commercial construction punishes sloppy schedules. Every day your space is offline is revenue you're not earning. We build commercial and tenant-improvement projects in Santa Cruz County with clear milestones, nightly cleanup, and the permit fluency to keep inspectors moving.

  • Retail build-outs — storefronts, showrooms, boutiques, specialty retail. Storefront glazing, accessible entries, POS and security rough-ins, display lighting.
  • Office tenant improvements — open-plan conversions, private-office buildouts, conference-room glass walls, new data and power drops, break rooms.
  • Medical and dental TIs — op rooms, sterilization areas, dedicated plumbing for chairs, lead-lined X-ray rooms, HIPAA-considerate layouts.
  • Professional services — law offices, accountants, therapists, salons. Sound-attenuated walls, private entries, ADA-compliant restrooms.
  • Mixed-use ground-floor commercial — shell-to-suite buildouts in new mixed-use buildings across downtown Santa Cruz and Capitola Village.
  • Façade and entry upgrades — storefront replacement, accessible entries, exterior signage support.

What separates commercial from residential

The building-code side changes fast. Commercial projects mean: California Building Code Chapter 11B accessibility compliance (path of travel, restroom clearances, counter heights, signage), Title 24 Part 6 commercial energy code, fire-and-life-safety review (occupant load, exiting, sprinklers, fire alarm), and often health-department review depending on use. We know which reviewers at Santa Cruz Building Division and Capitola Community Development flag what — and we design the submittal to pass first round, not third.

Working around an operating business

Most of our commercial work is in occupied buildings — adjacent tenants still need to open their doors. We plan for:

  • After-hours and weekend work for the loudest phases (demo, concrete cutting)
  • Sealed dust walls with negative-air HEPA filtration in healthcare and food spaces
  • Nightly cleanup so hallways and shared spaces read clean by morning
  • Loading-dock and freight-elevator scheduling that respects your landlord's rules
  • Clear communication with the property manager — we attend their weekly walkthrough if they have one

Budget ranges (2026)

  • Light office TI (paint, carpet, minor wall changes): $40–$80/sq ft
  • Standard office or retail buildout: $120–$250/sq ft
  • Medical or dental TI: $250–$500/sq ft
  • Full shell-to-suite with MEP: $200–$400/sq ft

Permit and schedule

Simple TIs that fit over-the-counter review can start construction 4–8 weeks from signed contract. Larger buildouts with full MEP and life-safety review run 10–20 weeks from contract to first day of demo. We give you a realistic calendar with the estimate — and we hit the dates we quote.

Who we work with

Commercial landlords, property managers, tenants negotiating new leases, and owner-users buying their own buildings. We're comfortable with landlord work letters, tenant allowances, and three-party coordination with architects and leasing brokers.

FAQ

Common questions about commercial

Can you work with our architect, or do you bring your own?

Both. Most commercial TIs come with a tenant's or landlord's architect already engaged — we work seamlessly with them. For smaller projects we can bring in a local commercial architect we've partnered with.

Do you handle ADA path-of-travel upgrades?

Yes — that's one of the most common scope items triggered by any commercial TI in California. We build it into the estimate up front so it isn't a surprise during plan check.

Can you do night or weekend work to keep the business open?

Yes. We've done phased projects where the business operated through construction, and after-hours jobs where all noisy work happened between 8pm and 6am. It costs a premium but it's often worth it.

Are you bondable?

Yes. Larger commercial projects that require performance or payment bonds — we can provide them through our surety.
