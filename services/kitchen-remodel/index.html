Kitchen Remodels in Santa Cruz, California

Why Santa Cruz kitchens are different

Santa Cruz homes span redwood cabins in Ben Lomond, Craftsman bungalows on the Westside, mid-century tract houses in Live Oak, and ocean-adjacent builds in Capitola and Aptos. Every one of those has kitchen quirks a templated remodeler misses — settling foundations in the hills, salt-air corrosion on hardware near the coast, undersized 1950s panels that can't handle an induction range, and original knob-and-tube that has to come out before drywall goes back on.

We plan for that up front so you don't get surprise change orders three weeks into demo.

What a Gravity kitchen remodel includes

  • Design consult — we walk the space with you, talk through how you actually cook and entertain, and sketch layout options (galley vs. L vs. island) with realistic cost impact for each.
  • Permits — pulled through City of Santa Cruz, County of Santa Cruz, or Capitola/Scotts Valley as appropriate. You never interact with the permit office; we do.
  • Demo and disposal — we protect flooring, seal off the rest of the house, and haul everything out clean.
  • Structural changes — wall removal with proper beam sizing, headers engineered and stamped when required.
  • Electrical — new circuits for range, dishwasher, disposal, microwave; GFCI/AFCI to current code; under-cabinet lighting; can lights; outlet placement that actually works for how you use the counter.
  • Plumbing — sink relocation, supply and drain updates, pot filler roughs, filtered or instant-hot water lines.
  • HVAC — range hood venting to exterior (not recirculated), any duct relocation.
  • Cabinetry — semi-custom or fully custom, installed tight and shimmed square. Soft-close everywhere.
  • Countertops — quartz, quartzite, granite, butcher block, soapstone — templated after cabinets are in, not before.
  • Tile + backsplash — proper substrate, waterproofing behind wet walls, grout color matched to stone.
  • Appliance install — ranges, refrigerators, wall ovens, vent hoods, wine fridges — all integrated cleanly.
  • Finish carpentry — crown, toe kicks, panel-ready fridge fronts, open shelving, custom pantry interiors.
  • Final walkthrough + warranty — we fix punch-list items on our dime, and we stand behind the work.

What does a kitchen remodel cost in Santa Cruz?

Ranges vary with scope, but as of 2026 a realistic ballpark in Santa Cruz County is:

  • Cosmetic refresh (paint, hardware, counters, backsplash, maybe appliances; same layout): $35,000–$65,000
  • Mid-range remodel (new cabinets and counters, some electrical/plumbing updates, same footprint): $65,000–$120,000
  • Full remodel with layout changes (wall removal, moved plumbing, island added, full appliance suite): $120,000–$250,000+

You'll get a detailed written estimate that breaks those numbers down line by line — no vague allowances that balloon mid-project.

Timeline

A typical Santa Cruz kitchen remodel runs 6–12 weeks from demo day to final walkthrough. Custom cabinetry lead times (8–16 weeks) usually set the pace; we start fabrication well before demo so cabinets arrive when the boxes are ready.

FAQ

Common questions about kitchen remodel

Can you work around me if I live in the house during the remodel?

Yes — we set up a temporary kitchen zone, seal off the work area with dust walls, and time water/electric shutoffs so you're never blindsided. Most clients stay in the home for a kitchen remodel.

Do I need a permit for my Santa Cruz kitchen remodel?

If you're changing plumbing locations, moving gas, adding electrical circuits, or removing walls — yes. We pull everything through the appropriate City or County permit office as part of the project.

Do you handle the design, or do I need a designer first?

Both options work. We do space-planning and materials selection in-house for most remodels. For whole-home-scale projects we partner with Santa Cruz-area designers and architects.
