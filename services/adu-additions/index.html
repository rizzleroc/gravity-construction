ADUs & additions

ADU Builders & Home Additions in Santa Cruz County

California's ADU laws make adding a second unit or expanding your home easier than it's ever been — but the local permit process still takes experience. We've navigated it across Santa Cruz, Capitola, Scotts Valley, and the unincorporated County.

What California's ADU laws unlocked

Under state law (and Santa Cruz County's ADU ordinance) most single-family lots can add at least one ADU up to 800–1,200 sq ft plus a junior ADU. Setbacks are relaxed, parking requirements are often waived, and the ministerial approval process means cities can't arbitrarily block well-designed projects. The upside: you can add rental income, a separate guest or in-law space, or future caregiver housing — all while materially boosting your property value.

The catch: every Santa Cruz County jurisdiction interprets the rules slightly differently, and the fire-department clearance, utility tie-in, and septic/sewer logistics are where projects bog down. That's the part experience saves you on.

Types of ADUs we build

  • Detached new-build ADU — ground-up structure on the same lot as the main house. Typically 400–1,200 sq ft. Best for lots with enough yard and utility access.
  • Attached ADU — addition with its own entrance that shares a wall with the main home. Often cheaper per sq ft because you're sharing foundation and some utilities.
  • Garage-conversion ADU — turning an existing attached or detached garage into a full living unit. Fastest path and often the lowest-cost, as long as the structure is sound.
  • Junior ADU (JADU) — up to 500 sq ft carved out of the main house, sharing a bathroom or kitchen access in some cases. Great for in-law suites.
  • Home additions — adding square footage to the main house (primary suite, larger kitchen, office) rather than creating a separate unit.

What's involved

A typical ADU timeline from first meeting to move-in day:

  1. Feasibility review (1–2 weeks) — we check your lot against the current ADU ordinance, flag setback/utility/slope issues early
  2. Design (4–10 weeks) — working with you and a local architect or designer on plans that maximize the unit's value without overbuilding
  3. Permit submittal (3–6 months for approval, depending on jurisdiction and project complexity)
  4. Site prep + foundation (2–4 weeks)
  5. Framing, roofing, rough-ins (6–10 weeks)
  6. Finishes + final inspections (6–10 weeks)

Start-to-move-in: 10–16 months for a detached ADU; 6–10 months for a garage conversion.

ADU cost in Santa Cruz County

As of 2026, budget ranges we see on real projects:

  • Garage-conversion ADU: $180,000–$300,000
  • Detached 400–600 sq ft ADU: $350,000–$500,000
  • Detached 800–1,200 sq ft ADU: $500,000–$800,000
  • Primary-suite addition: $180,000–$400,000

Ranges depend heavily on finishes, site conditions (hillside lots and septic systems cost more), and whether you're keeping existing utilities or bringing in new service.

FAQ

Common questions about adu / addition

Can I rent out my ADU on Airbnb?

Short-term rental rules vary by jurisdiction. City of Santa Cruz, for example, restricts non-hosted short-term rentals. County of Santa Cruz has its own rules. We'll flag the limitations for your lot during the feasibility review.

Do I need to add parking for the ADU?

Under current state law, no new parking is required for ADUs on lots within a half-mile of transit or in historic districts. Many Santa Cruz lots qualify. We verify during feasibility.

How does an ADU affect my property taxes?

California's Prop 19 means only the new ADU square footage is reassessed — your existing home's tax basis stays intact. Check with your accountant for specifics.

Can I finance an ADU?

Yes — construction loans, HELOCs, and CalHFA's ADU grant programs are all common paths. We can recommend local lenders who do a lot of ADU work.
