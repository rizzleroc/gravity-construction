What new construction in Santa Cruz actually involves

Ground-up construction is the hardest thing a builder does. On a Santa Cruz County lot you're likely dealing with some combination of: hillside grading and drainage, coastal commission or riparian setbacks, Title 24 all-electric code (as of 2023), seismic bracing for slope, stormwater management, oak-tree protection zones, and fire-hardening requirements in WUI (wildland-urban interface) zones. Plus the baseline — foundation, framing, MEP, finishes.

We work through it all with you: site evaluation, civil engineer referrals, structural engineering, Title 24 energy compliance, permit shepherding, and then the actual build with the owner on site every phase.

Our process

  1. Site walk + feasibility — we look at your lot before you design anything. Constraints drive design; getting them wrong costs you six figures.
  2. Team assembly — architect, civil engineer, structural engineer, soils engineer if needed. We have local Santa Cruz-area professionals we work with regularly.
  3. Design development — floor plans, exteriors, initial material choices. You iterate until it's right.
  4. Pre-construction services — detailed cost estimate, schedule, permit strategy. This is where we price real, not guess.
  5. Permit submittal and approval — typically 6–12 months in Santa Cruz County; coastal zone or hillside adds time.
  6. Construction — site prep, foundation, framing, rough-ins, shell complete, finishes, final inspections, certificate of occupancy. Usually 10–16 months.

What we build well

  • Modern coastal homes with durable detailing for salt air
  • Redwood mountain homes with defensible space and fire-hardening
  • Contemporary hillside builds with steel-and-concrete structural systems
  • Small-footprint, well-built custom homes for infill lots

Budget ranges (2026)

Every lot is different, but as a sanity-check:

  • Entry custom (simple lot, standard finishes): $600–$800 per sq ft
  • Mid-range custom (most projects): $800–$1,100 per sq ft
  • High-end custom (architectural, complex sites, premium finishes): $1,100–$1,600+ per sq ft

Site-prep costs — grading, drainage, utilities, driveway — can add $75,000 to $500,000 on top of the house itself, depending on lot difficulty.

Timeline reality

From the day you close on a lot to the day you move in, Santa Cruz County new construction is usually 18–30 months. Shorter for flat, serviced lots with straightforward designs. Longer for coastal-zone, hillside, or permit-complicated projects. We'll build a realistic schedule from day one — not a fantasy one.

FAQ

Common questions about new construction

Do I need an architect?

For a ground-up custom home in Santa Cruz County, essentially yes — you need stamped plans. We partner with local architects or can work from plans you bring to us.

Are you all-electric, per California's 2023 code?

Yes. New construction in California is now all-electric by default — heat pumps for HVAC and water heating, induction ranges. We've been building this way since the code switched.

Can you do coastal commission work?

Yes, we've built projects that required Coastal Development Permits. It's a process, but a predictable one when you know the path.
