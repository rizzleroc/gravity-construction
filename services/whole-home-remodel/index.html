Whole-home remodels

Whole-Home Remodels in Santa Cruz, California

A whole-home remodel is the chance to fix everything at once — the systems you hate, the layout that never worked, the finishes you grew out of. We handle them end-to-end: one contract, one supervisor, one clear path.

When a whole-home remodel is the right call

If three or more of these apply to your house, you're probably better off with a whole-home remodel than a series of disconnected projects:

  • Kitchen and at least one bath both need work
  • Electrical panel is undersized or the wiring is knob-and-tube, aluminum, or old cloth-sheathed
  • Plumbing is galvanized or polybutylene
  • Layout doesn't match how you live — walls in the wrong places
  • Windows are single-pane or failing
  • No central HVAC, or the existing system is 20+ years old
  • You're planning to stay in the house 10+ years

Tackling these as one project saves you duplicate permit fees, duplicate mobilization costs, and the hassle of living through half a dozen separate construction cycles.

What's involved

  • Gut demo or targeted demo depending on scope — we protect floors and fixtures we're keeping
  • System updates: new panel and circuits, PEX or copper replumb, high-efficiency heat pump HVAC, Title-24 compliant insulation and air sealing
  • Structural: wall removal, seismic retrofits where foundations lack proper connection (common in pre-1970s Santa Cruz homes), subfloor repair
  • Layout changes: opening up kitchen-to-living, moving primary bedroom for privacy, adding walk-in closets, improving indoor-outdoor flow
  • Windows and doors: dual-pane low-E throughout, proper flashing and water management
  • Exterior: siding repair or replacement, deck rebuild, landscaping prep
  • All new finishes: floors, trim, doors, paint, tile, fixtures, appliances, cabinetry

How we keep whole-home remodels sane

These are big projects. Two things separate a good one from a nightmare:

  1. A detailed, realistic estimate up front. We spec materials down to the fixture and tile before we give you a number — no "allowances" that balloon 40%.
  2. A single owner-supervisor on site. Damien walks the job every day. When something comes up — and on a whole-home remodel, it always does — a decision gets made fast and the job keeps moving.

Budget and timeline

Ranges for a typical Santa Cruz County whole-home remodel:

  • 1,500 sq ft mid-range: $400,000–$700,000, 6–10 months
  • 2,500 sq ft mid-to-high: $700,000–$1,500,000, 10–14 months
  • 3,500+ sq ft, high-end + structural: $1.5M–$3M+, 14–20 months

Most clients move out for the bulk of the work — it's faster for us, and less miserable for you.

FAQ

Common questions about whole-home remodel

Is a whole-home remodel cheaper than tearing down and rebuilding?

Usually yes, if the existing foundation and framing are sound. New construction hits $800–$1,400/sq ft; a whole-home remodel is typically $300–$700/sq ft. We'll honestly tell you when a teardown would serve you better.

Do we have to move out?

For whole-home scope, yes — typically for 6–14 months. We help you plan the transition. A few clients stay with extended family, others rent nearby.

Can we keep the exterior the same and only remodel the inside?

Absolutely. Many of our whole-home remodels leave the exterior envelope and just touch up siding and windows while gutting the interior.
