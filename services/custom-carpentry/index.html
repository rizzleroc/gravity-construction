Custom carpentry

Custom Finish Carpentry in Santa Cruz

The built-in bookcase that fits like it was always there. The wainscoting that matches the 1920s profile you inherited. The floor-to-ceiling pantry that hides the mess. Custom finish carpentry is where a house starts to feel custom.

What we build

  • Built-in bookcases and entertainment walls — sized to the room, trimmed to existing architecture
  • Window seats and reading nooks — with hinged lids for storage or drawers underneath
  • Wainscoting, beadboard, board-and-batten — matched to historic profiles on older homes, clean modern lines on contemporary ones
  • Custom pantries — pull-outs, drawer banks, sliding shelves, lazy susans
  • Mudrooms and drop zones — benches, cubbies, hooks, shoe storage
  • Closet systems — custom reach-ins and walk-ins with drawers, shelving, shoe racks, and hanging sections sized to your actual wardrobe
  • Crown molding, baseboards, door and window casings — matched to existing trim or designed new
  • Interior doors and hardware — solid-core replacements, pocket doors, barn doors on hardware
  • Stair rebuilds and custom railings — wood, iron, cable, or glass
  • Fireplace surrounds and mantels
  • Wet bars and coffee stations

Why on-site, not shop-built?

For built-ins, on-site fabrication almost always wins. Rooms are never perfectly square, floors are never perfectly flat, and corners are never exactly 90°. A built-in fabricated in a shop has to deal with all of that on install day — often with visible gaps or awkward scribes. On-site, we dry-fit as we go, scribe the face to the wall, and hang the finish so the piece reads as architecture, not furniture pushed against the wall.

For straightforward cabinet-box work (pantries, closets, some entertainment walls), shop-built boxes with on-site face frames and finish carpentry is the right blend of speed and fit. We pick the right approach per project.

Typical budgets

  • Small built-in or window seat: $3,500–$8,000
  • Full wall of built-ins with shelves, cabinets, and lighting: $12,000–$28,000
  • Whole-house trim package (base, case, crown on a 2,000 sq ft home): $18,000–$40,000
  • Stair rebuild with custom railing: $15,000–$45,000
  • Kitchen pantry build-out: $5,000–$15,000

FAQ

Common questions about custom carpentry

Do you do built-ins as a standalone project, or only as part of a bigger remodel?

Both. We take standalone carpentry projects regularly — a wall of built-ins, a stair rebuild, a trim package.

Can you match existing trim profiles in an older home?

Yes. We take samples, source matching or custom-milled stock, and blend new work into original trim so you can't tell where old ends and new begins.
