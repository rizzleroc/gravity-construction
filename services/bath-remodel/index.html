Bath remodels

Bathroom Remodels in Santa Cruz, California

Bathrooms are where sloppy work comes back to haunt you. We build Santa Cruz bathroom remodels to last — with real waterproofing, proper slope, and plumbing that'll pass inspection the first time.

Start a Project Call (831) 234-4669

Built to survive coastal moisture

Near the Santa Cruz coast, salt air and high humidity eat cheap fixtures in two years. We spec corrosion-resistant hardware, use closed-cell foam or mineral wool in wet-wall cavities, and waterproof with Schluter Kerdi or RedGard systems behind every tile shower — not just the old-school tar paper that gets you through the final but fails at year three.

What's included

  • Full demo to studs where needed — we find and fix any existing water damage before new tile goes up
  • Plumbing rough-in: valve replacement, new shower rough, sink and toilet relocation if layout is changing
  • Electrical: GFCI circuits, in-floor heat wiring, fan and light on separate switches, dedicated vanity outlets
  • Waterproofing: membrane-based systems (Schluter/RedGard) with proper lapping onto the pan and over curbs
  • Shower pans: poured mud, linear drains, curbless walk-in builds when the subfloor allows
  • Tile: large-format porcelain, subway, Zellige, mosaics — set flat with proper lippage and grout joint sizing
  • Vanity and counters: custom or semi-custom with stone or quartz tops
  • Heated floors: Nuheat or Warmup mats under tile — a small upcharge that pays back fast
  • Ventilation: properly sized exhaust fan vented to exterior, not into the attic (which violates code and grows mold)

Popular Santa Cruz bath remodel types

  • Hall bath refresh ($15,000–$35,000) — tub-to-shower conversion, new vanity, tile, fixtures
  • Primary bath remodel ($40,000–$90,000) — custom walk-in shower, freestanding tub, double vanity, heated floors
  • Aging-in-place conversion ($20,000–$55,000) — curbless shower, grab-bar backing, comfort-height toilet, lever handles
  • Full primary suite build-out ($75,000–$150,000+) — layout changes, reconfigured closet, window additions

Timeline

A straightforward bath remodel is 3–6 weeks. Primary baths with custom shower builds, heated floors, and vanity fabrication run 6–10 weeks.

Why waterproofing matters more than anything else

We've done plenty of remodels where we've torn out previous "remodels" from other contractors and found mold behind the tile, rotted subfloor under the shower pan, and fans vented into the attic instead of outside. Those are the kinds of shortcuts that save the other guy a day and cost you $15,000 to fix four years later. We don't take them.

FAQ

Common questions about bath remodel

Can you do a tub-to-shower conversion?

Yes — very common request. We remove the tub, rebuild the curb/pan, install new waterproofing, and finish in tile or solid surface. A standard hall-bath conversion takes about 3 weeks.

What's the difference between a $15k and a $50k bath remodel?

Scope and materials. A $15k job is typically a cosmetic update in an existing footprint with stock fixtures. A $50k job often includes layout changes, custom tile work, heated floors, and higher-end fixtures and stone. We'll help you match scope to budget honestly.

Do you handle the plumbing permit?

Yes — every permit is pulled under our license. You never deal with the County or City directly.
