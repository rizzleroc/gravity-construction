General Contractor in Ben Lomond, CA

Ben Lomond and the rest of the San Lorenzo Valley are where we take on the county's most character-rich — and most challenging — remodels. Mountain homes, steep driveways, big redwoods, and post-2020-fire sensibilities.

Mountain construction is different

Ben Lomond work comes with access challenges you don't see down on the flats: one-lane driveways, 20% slopes, 80-foot redwoods we have to protect, and winter road closures that can shut down a site for a week. We scope all of that upfront so it doesn't turn into surprise change orders.

Ben Lomond is unincorporated Santa Cruz County. Much of it is designated WUI (Wildland-Urban Interface), which means exterior work has to meet Chapter 7A ignition-resistance standards. Many of our clients here are either fire-rebuilders or owners hardening their homes against the next fire.

Fire-wise construction

We don't just meet WUI code — we go past it where the cost difference is small. Triple-pane windows, fully enclosed eaves, hardboard or metal siding, Class A roofs, non-combustible decks, and 100-foot defensible space planning. Insurance carriers are increasingly rewarding these specs with better rates, and the peace of mind during a red-flag day is worth it on its own.

Winter staging

We don't start foundation pours in the SLV in January or February unless there's no alternative — the rain risk is too high. For mountain clients we typically plan permit in fall, demo and foundation in late spring, and finish in fall of the following year.

FAQ

Common questions — Ben Lomond

Do you take on fire-rebuild projects?

Yes. We've worked with owners rebuilding after the CZU complex and can coordinate with your insurance adjuster, the county's rebuild-streamlining program, and the geotech reports those rebuilds require.

Can you protect the redwoods on my lot?

Absolutely. Critical Root Zone protection is part of our standard site-prep plan — we fence the drip line, require no-grade zones, and coordinate with a certified arborist when needed.
