Remodeling in Santa Cruz takes a contractor who knows the city

Santa Cruz homes are a patchwork — 1910 Craftsman bungalows on the Westside, 1940s beach cottages near Twin Lakes, post-war ranches off Soquel, and modern infill from the last decade. Each era brings its own surprises once the drywall comes off: knob-and-tube wiring, galvanized supply lines, unpermitted additions stacked by three previous owners, redwood sheathing that's beautiful but dry as a bone. We've opened up walls in every one of these vintages and know what to expect.

The City of Santa Cruz also has its own Building & Safety process, its own historic review in certain overlay zones, and a Coastal Commission zone that covers everything south of Mission Street down to the water. Permits in Santa Cruz move faster when the drawings anticipate city staff's questions — we prepare submittals with that in mind so your project doesn't get stuck in corrections cycles.

Permits and inspections in the City of Santa Cruz

The City's Planning & Community Development Department runs permit submittals through an online portal (EnerGov). For kitchen and bath remodels, expect mechanical, electrical, and plumbing inspections plus a final. For ADUs, the state ministerial track (SB 9 / ADU streamlining) generally applies — 60-day review once the application is complete. Coastal zone projects add a Coastal Development Permit review, which is the single biggest schedule variable we see on Westside jobs.

We pull all permits in the owner's name or ours depending on what makes sense for your lender and insurance. Either way, you get copies of every approved drawing set and every inspection card.

What we build most often in the city

Kitchen remodels are the number-one request — usually opening up the wall between the kitchen and dining room, replacing original tile counters with quartz or soapstone, and upgrading the electrical panel since the existing one is almost always at capacity. Bath remodels run a close second; waterproofing is where we don't cut corners, because Santa Cruz humidity punishes sloppy pan details within a year.

ADUs are booming. The state now allows up to 1,200 sq ft detached and the city's fee schedule is predictable. Most of our ADU clients are building for aging parents, adult kids, or long-term rental income.

FAQ

Common questions — Santa Cruz

Do you work in the Coastal Zone?

Yes. We've completed projects throughout the Santa Cruz Coastal Zone and are familiar with the Coastal Development Permit process. Expect an additional 60–120 days on the schedule when a CDP is required; we build that into the timeline upfront.

How long does a kitchen remodel take in Santa Cruz?

A typical mid-range kitchen remodel runs 8–12 weeks on site, plus 4–6 weeks of design and permitting. Historic-district homes or projects with structural changes can push to 14–16 weeks.

Can you build an ADU on my Westside lot?

In most cases yes. California state law preempts a lot of local restrictions — setbacks as tight as 4 feet, no off-street parking required near transit, and ministerial approval. We'll check your specific APN for overlay zones before committing.
