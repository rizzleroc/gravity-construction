Service area

General Contractor in Live Oak, CA

Live Oak runs from the Santa Cruz city limit at Rodriguez Street down past Pleasure Point to the Capitola line. It's the densest unincorporated area in the county and the one where we see the most variety in our Monday schedule.

Start a Project Call (831) 234-4669

Live Oak is where Santa Cruz meets beach town

Homes near Pleasure Point were built as weekend cottages in the 1940s and 1950s and are now year-round residences getting their first or second serious remodel. Lots are small, street parking is short, and surfers walking by at 6 AM is a scheduling consideration we actually factor in when planning deliveries.

Live Oak is unincorporated, so permits go through Santa Cruz County. Most of the Live Oak south of East Cliff Drive falls within the Coastal Zone — expect a CDP review for any exterior work, which adds 2–4 months to the permit timeline.

Areas we cover in Live Oak

  • Pleasure Point — Surfer-cottage remodels, outdoor showers, board storage, salt-spec finishes.
  • East Cliff Drive — Oceanfront homes with bluff-setback considerations and heavy weather exposure.
  • 17th Avenue & the Simpkins area — Post-war tract homes, first-time major remodels, kitchen and bath packages.
  • Live Oak Elementary / Brommer Street — Family homes with ADU or addition needs as kids get older.
  • Twin Lakes & Harbor edge — Boat-adjacent properties, storage-heavy garage remodels, ADUs used as rental income.

Coastal Commission timing

On any Live Oak project south of East Cliff, we submit the county permit and the CDP in parallel rather than sequentially. It's the single biggest schedule optimization we've found for Live Oak work.

FAQ

Common questions — Live Oak

Do I need a Coastal Development Permit for an interior remodel?

Usually not. Interior-only work that doesn't change the building footprint or add bedrooms typically doesn't trigger a CDP. Exterior work, additions, and ADUs usually do.
