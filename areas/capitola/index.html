General Contractor in Capitola, CA

Capitola's tight lots, historic charm, and literal oceanfront exposure make it one of the most rewarding — and least forgiving — places we build. We've done everything from Jewel Box cottage refreshes to Depot Hill whole-home remodels.

Building in a village where every inch counts

Capitola Village is platted on a grid that dates to 1869. Lots are narrow, setbacks are short, and many of the original cottages sit on rubble foundations or un-reinforced post-and-pier. When we take on a Capitola remodel, the first thing we do is a structural walk-through — because you can't bolt modern cabinetry and tile to a foundation that's six inches off plumb without fixing the underlying issue.

The City of Capitola has its own planning counter, its own design review in the Village overlay, and shares Coastal Commission jurisdiction with the county. Historic preservation matters here; the wrong window shape on a Village-zoned cottage can kill a permit faster than any other single factor.

Neighborhoods we work in

  • Capitola Village — Beach cottages with heavy salt exposure, narrow-lot kitchen remodels, exterior work that has to pass Village design review.
  • Jewel Box — Pastel-painted cottages that owners want to modernize inside without losing the curb appeal. Interior-only remodels are our sweet spot here.
  • Depot Hill — Ocean-view homes with the harshest wind-and-salt exposure in the county. Finish selection matters; we steer clients toward marine-grade hardware and thermally broken window systems.
  • 41st Avenue corridor — Mid-century tract homes getting their first or second major remodel. Electrical and plumbing are always in the scope.
  • New Brighton & McGregor Drive — Larger lots, more freedom on additions and ADUs, but hillside grading rules apply.

Coastal exposure is not a talking point — it's a material spec

In Capitola we default to stainless 316 fasteners, marine-grade plywood substrates in wet areas, fiber-cement or composite siding rather than wood, and fiberglass or aluminum-clad windows over vinyl. These upgrades add 5–10% to a finish budget but pay back in decades of not having to redo the work. Clients who come to us after a cheaper contractor used galvanized hardware usually regret the savings within three years.

Permits in Capitola

The city processes permits through its own small Building Department; response times are generally 3–6 weeks for a standard remodel and 8–12 weeks for Village-overlay projects that need design review. We handle the submittal and the back-and-forth with staff so you're not dropping by City Hall during your lunch break.

FAQ

Common questions — Capitola

Can I add a second story to my Capitola Village cottage?

Sometimes — it depends on your lot's zoning and whether the structure can support it. Many Village cottages are best as one-story remodels with rear additions. We'll pull zoning and do a structural check before promising anything.

What does salt-air exposure actually cost in a remodel?

Roughly 5–10% more on finishes and hardware, depending on how close you are to the water. The alternative is re-doing corroded fasteners and rusted anchors within 3–5 years.
