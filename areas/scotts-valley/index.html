General Contractor in Scotts Valley, CA

Scotts Valley is its own incorporated city with its own permit counter, its own design tastes, and a housing stock that ranges from 1970s planned developments to modern estate properties on the Glenwood ridge.

Start a Project Call (831) 234-4669

Scotts Valley homes are bigger, newer, and more forgiving

Most Scotts Valley remodels we do are second-phase improvements on homes built between 1975 and 2005 — updating dated oak cabinets, reconfiguring formal living-dining plans into open kitchen-family-room layouts, and adding primary suites. The framing is predictable, the electrical is to code, and the plumbing is copper and PEX. Surprises are fewer; aesthetics take the spotlight.

Scotts Valley has its own Building Department and its own design review. Permits are generally faster than unincorporated county work because the city's scale lets plan review stay responsive.

Areas we cover in Scotts Valley

  • Glenwood Drive & the ridge — Estate-scale remodels, outdoor living, view-preserving additions.
  • Graham Hill & Mount Hermon Road — Tract and semi-custom homes, workflow-focused kitchen remodels, primary suite additions.
  • El Pueblo & Kings Village — Older neighborhoods with a mix of additions, interior remodels, and ADU work.
  • Scotts Valley Drive corridor — 1990s–2000s homes, high-end kitchen remodels, accessory office builds.

Fire code matters here

Much of Scotts Valley falls within the Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) zone. That means Chapter 7A of the California Building Code applies: ignition-resistant siding, ember-resistant vents, Class A roof assemblies, and non-combustible decking within 10 feet of the structure. We spec to WUI by default on any exterior work in Scotts Valley and won't let a client talk us out of it.

FAQ

Common questions — Scotts Valley

What is a WUI zone and does it apply to me?

Wildland-Urban Interface — areas where development meets wildfire-prone vegetation. Most of Scotts Valley, the San Lorenzo Valley, and parts of Aptos Hills are mapped WUI. If yours is, exterior materials must meet ignition-resistance standards. We'll check before quoting.

Can you match the style of my 1990s Scotts Valley home?

Yes — and we'll also show you respectful updates that modernize without turning a transitional home into a farmhouse caricature. Our work tends to go with the bones of the house.
