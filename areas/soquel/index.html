General Contractor in Soquel, CA

Soquel sits at the crossroads of Santa Cruz County — close enough to the coast to feel the breeze, far enough inland to escape the fog. We remodel across the entire 95073 zip, from the Village to the top of Old San Jose Road.

Start a Project Call (831) 234-4669

Soquel's mix of housing stock keeps the work interesting

You'll find 1970s ranches, 1920s Craftsmans in the Village, 1990s tract homes off Soquel Drive, and multi-acre Old San Jose Road properties all within a three-mile radius. Our Soquel projects run the full range: $80K kitchen refreshes, $350K ADUs, $600K whole-home remodels on Porter Gulch.

Soquel is unincorporated — permits go through Santa Cruz County. The permit experience is the same as Aptos and Live Oak: file through the County portal, expect a standard remodel to clear plan check in 4–8 weeks, longer if any hillside or riparian issues are involved.

Areas we cover in Soquel

  • Soquel Village — Historic core, character homes with older systems, careful about neighborhood compatibility.
  • Soquel Drive corridor — Mid-century to late-20th-century stock, often first-time major remodels.
  • Old San Jose Road — Semi-rural, larger lots, well and septic work coordinated with our subs.
  • Porter Gulch & Cherryvale — Hillside parcels with geotech considerations and longer driveways (staging matters).
  • Mar Vista & 41st Avenue edge — Tract homes sharing characteristics with Capitola; kitchen and bath remodels are frequent.

Common Soquel projects

Kitchen remodels where the cook wants an island but the existing layout is a galley. Primary suite additions on homes that were built with tiny bedrooms by modern standards. Detached ADUs built as home offices or for renting — the Soquel rental market is strong and stable. Workshop-garage builds for the many Soquel residents who run trade businesses out of their homes.

FAQ

Common questions — Soquel

Can you work on a rural Soquel property with a well and septic?

Yes. We coordinate with licensed well and septic contractors and pull Environmental Health permits when the project adds bedrooms or plumbing load. Septic capacity is often the limiting factor on ADU builds, so we check it early.
